# H8 — streaming Kimodo flesh transfer

## Question

Do the frozen H7C mechanics produce bounded, coherent secondary motion when
driven from a cold state by full, nonperiodic Kimodo clips, or did their success
depend on the warmed periodic walk/cycle protocol used through H7D?

H8 is a motion-generalization and runtime-state experiment. It does not retrain
the H6C backbone, H7C density residual, Kimodo, the retarget map, or the body.

## Frozen mechanics

- H6C's five identified constitutive coefficients are loaded from
  `experiments/runs/h6c_final/metrics.json`.
- H7C's three bounded density-residual checkpoints are loaded from
  `experiments/runs/h7c_initial/seed{7,19,31}`.
- H7C pressure/cohesion coefficients, five-percent compression and eight-percent
  stretch dead zones, four substeps at 30 fps, and the `12 m/s²` smooth density
  cap remain unchanged.
- The 91,979-cell, 12.5 mm H5U body, six skin weights, graph topology, texture,
  and splat settings remain unchanged.

## Streaming protocol

Each accepted retarget artifact supplies its complete 30 fps LBS trajectory.
There is no truncation, palindrome closure, cycle repetition, or teacher phase
initialization. Explicit teacher, H6C backbone control, and H7C hybrid all begin
with residual position and residual velocity exactly zero.

Skeleton acceleration uses nonperiodic finite differences. After the generated
clip, the final pose is repeated for 30 frames. This one-second hold tests whether
secondary motion damps rather than drifting or oscillating indefinitely.

Every clip has two frozen timing arms:

1. `natural`: the full generated frame sequence at 30 fps;
2. `2x`: deterministic linear time resampling to half the motion duration at the
   same 30 fps, followed by the same 30-frame hold.

The density teacher is simulated from the same cold state and is used only as a
synthetic mechanics oracle. It is not biological ground truth.

## Arms

- **LBS:** zero residual; rig and retarget control.
- **Backbone:** frozen H6C constitutive dynamics with density disabled.
- **Hybrid:** frozen H6C plus one frozen H7C bounded density checkpoint.
- **Teacher:** explicit H7C graph-elastic and density forces, used for metrics.

The primary visual artifact places LBS beside hybrid for the same frames and
camera. Backbone and teacher remain quantitative controls.

## Per-arm metrics and gates

The periodic seam/drift gates are inapplicable. H8 replaces them with cold-start
and final-hold measurements while retaining H7's absolute rollout safeguards.

Every clip/timing/checkpoint must pass:

- teacher and rollout states are finite;
- teacher residual RMS is between `0.5 mm` and `75 mm`;
- teacher and predicted density acceleration never exceed `12.0001 m/s²`;
- frozen density one-step acceleration NRMSE is at most `0.15`;
- hybrid teacher-relative position RMS is at most `4 mm`, p99 at most `12 mm`,
  and maximum at most `40 mm`;
- hybrid/teacher residual-amplitude ratio is between `0.70` and `1.30`;
- hybrid far/near residual-amplitude ratio remains within `±10%` of the explicit
  teacher's ratio;
- hybrid final residual RMS is at most `20 mm`; and
- after the one-second hold, hybrid residual-velocity RMS is at most the larger
  of `35%` of hold-entry velocity and `5 mm/s`.

A variant is **causally eligible** when teacher density acceleration RMS is at
least `0.02 m/s²` and backbone teacher-relative error is at least `0.2 mm`.
Every eligible variant must reduce backbone position error by at least 60% and
sampled compression error by at least 50%. Low-excitation variants receive an
ecological safety verdict rather than being mislabeled as causal evidence.

## Aggregate acceptance

Qualification passes when both opened clips pass every safety gate at natural
and 2x timing for seed 7. After that result is persisted, the final suite may be
generated and opened.

H8 passes only if:

- every final clip/timing/checkpoint passes all safety gates;
- every causally eligible final variant passes both causal gates; and
- at least three of the six final clip/timing variants are causally eligible.

The three-variant floor prevents a suite of gentle, vacuous motions from being
reported as mechanics generalization.

## Pre-final amendment 1 — softness profile

The first qualification run exposed an invalid absolute gate, not a mechanics
failure. The `standing_wave_2x` explicit teacher had far/near residual-amplitude
ratio `1.00562`; its hybrid prediction reproduced `1.00559` with `0.002 mm`
teacher-relative RMS error. The originally frozen gate nevertheless required
the hybrid ratio to exceed `1.15`, which could only pass by departing from the
teacher.

Before generating or inspecting any final motion, that gate was replaced with
the teacher-relative profile-preservation gate above. All other safety and
causal thresholds, mechanics, checkpoints, prompts, and seeds remain frozen.
The unchanged qualification suite must be rerun from scratch.

## Results

The unchanged qualification rerun passes all four motion/timing arms. Natural
and 2x two-step motion are causally eligible: the frozen hybrid reduces backbone
position error from `1.994` to `0.022 mm` and from `4.501` to `0.139 mm`,
respectively. Both standing-wave arms pass ecological safety; the natural arm
is low-excitation and the 2x arm remains below the frozen `0.2 mm` backbone
eligibility floor.

## Interpretation limits

A pass establishes cold-start, nonperiodic, cross-motion transfer against the
same synthetic material teacher, with bounded settling. It does not prove
biological realism, changing particle neighborhoods, collision/contact tissue,
muscle actuation, or a learned body model. A linearly skinned preview remains
the correct retarget control even after hybrid visuals are added.
