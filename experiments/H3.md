# H3 — fine humanoid envelope and learned fate

## Question

Can the sparse-flesh substrate scale from the five-bone H to a recognizable
human precursor with substantially finer splats and anatomy that is not a
nearest-bone offset? Can a learned allocator replace H2's hand-written regional
choice while preserving remote wound repair?

## Predeclared scaffold and morphology

- A connected 15-bone scaffold: trunk, neck/head axis, bilateral clavicle,
  upper/lower arm, hip, thigh, and lower-leg chains.
- A 3D tissue envelope containing torso, pelvis, head, hands, and feet in
  addition to limb capsules.
- Explicit left/right cheek and buttock lobes whose volume is offset from the
  nearest bone. These are critical regions, not decorative render additions.
- Reference spacing at most `0.10`, versus H2's `0.14`.
- Base rendered splat radius at most `0.15 × spacing`, versus H2's `0.20 ×
  spacing`, with a per-material scale range of at least 1.5:1.
- A predeclared wound in the right cheek. Replacement cells are fed from below,
  making the damaged region deliberately remote from their source.

The checker remains a diagnostic material chart. It is not claimed to be a
production humanoid UV map.

## Learned component

H2's exact site identities remain forbidden. Its seven-way hand-written region
selector is replaced by a small shared MLP that sees:

- normalized unfilled capacity for every anatomical region; and
- the cell's distance to every anatomical region.

The MLP emits one coarse region commitment. Within that region, position stays
continuous and is governed by live deficit attention plus density pressure.
The commitment is hysteretic. Training labels come from the frozen H2-style
capacity-and-distance oracle over randomized shortage/distance states. This is
oracle distillation, not yet end-to-end neural morphogenesis, and must be
reported as such.

## Arms

1. **Oracle fine-humanoid upper bound:** deterministic regional allocation,
   continuous within-region placement, spatial density, and plastic material.
2. **Learned fate (primary):** the trained MLP replaces only regional choice.
3. **Local deficit:** no coarse region commitment.
4. **Learned fate without shortage:** the allocator receives no live regional
   capacity, testing whether geometry/distance alone can repair the cheek.

All arms use the same morphology, particle count, fine splats, motion, and
damage/re-feed protocol. The neural acceleration residual remains zero so fate
learning is not confounded with motion learning.

## Protocol

1. Train the fate MLP on synthetic oracle states and freeze it.
2. Feed one target mass of generation-0 cells below the feet and assemble at
   rest.
3. Lock the mature tissue and measure the intact humanoid.
4. Delete cells occupying the right-cheek wound and measure the damage.
5. Feed exactly the removed mass as generation 1 and require local maturation.
6. Articulate the repaired scaffold through a bounded walk/sway cycle.

Only seed 7 may be used for dynamics tuning. After freezing the configuration,
seeds 19 and 31 are validation runs.

## Acceptance criteria

### Representation and learner

- At least 15 bones and 12 anatomical regions.
- Reference spacing no greater than 0.10.
- Maximum world-space rendered splat radius no greater than 75% of H2's
  (`0.20 × 0.14`) and material splat-scale ratio at least 1.5.
- At least 8% of reference tissue lies more than `0.18` world units from its
  nearest bone, establishing meaningful extra-skeletal volume.
- Frozen fate-model holdout agreement with the oracle at least 0.97.

### Oracle upper bound

- Pre-wound total coverage at least 0.80, every region at least 0.55, and mean
  critical-lobe coverage at least 0.70.
- Moving coverage at least 0.74, mean tracking error at most 0.12, crowding
  below 0.08, and material accuracy at least 0.78.

### Learned primary

- Pre-wound coverage at least 0.78 and within 0.06 of the oracle arm.
- Every region at least 0.50 covered; mean cheek/buttock coverage at least 0.65.
- Damage lowers right-cheek wound coverage by at least 0.30.
- Repair restores wound coverage to at least 0.70 and within 0.18 of its
  pre-wound value while healthy coverage loses no more than 0.10.
- At least 0.70 of generation-1 cells localize to the wound; at least 0.85
  commit and locally lock.
- Moving coverage at least 0.72, tracking error at most 0.13, crowding below
  0.08, and checker accuracy at least 0.75.
- Every active cell has a learned coarse guide, every `assigned_site` remains
  `-1`, mass is accounted for, and all state is finite.

## Results

### Frozen representation

The generated body has 15 bones, 18 nonempty anatomical regions, and 1,092
reference cells at 0.10 spacing. Cheek, buttock, torso, pelvis, head, hand, and
foot ellipsoids augment tapered limb capsules. The representation gates pass:

| Measurement | H3 value | Gate |
|---|---:|---:|
| Bones | 15 | at least 15 |
| Regions | 18 | at least 12 |
| Spacing | 0.100 | at most 0.100 |
| Maximum world splat radius | 0.0195 | at most 0.0210 |
| Material splat-scale ratio | 1.625 | at least 1.5 |
| Extra-skeletal tissue fraction | 0.450 | at least 0.08 |

The maximum rendered radius is 69.6% of H2's 0.028 world-unit radius. The
extra-skeletal measurement counts reference cells more than 0.18 units from
every bone; it is computed from the developmental body plan rather than the
rendered silhouette.

### Causal controls

Controls use seed 7 and the frozen configuration:

| Arm | Pre coverage | Min. region | Pre wound | Repaired wound | Moving coverage | Guide allocation error | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Learned fate | 0.908 | 0.833 | 0.875 | 0.875 | 0.855 | 0.000 | pass |
| Local deficit, no guide | 0.662 | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.663 | 1.000 | remote regions never assemble |
| Same MLP, shortage hidden | 0.125 | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.137 | 0.938 | distance captures cells near the feed source |

The shortage-blind arm retains the same trained weights and 0.987 model
holdout score; only live shortage and availability are hidden. Its collapse
therefore establishes that the global demand signal, rather than model capacity
alone, causes balanced anatomy and repair.

### Iteration record

Only seed 7 was used for tuning:

1. The oracle fine-humanoid arm passed immediately, validating geometry before
   changing the regional selector.
2. With H2's deficit log weight 2.2, the learned arm passed every gate except
   relative wound recovery: 13 of 16 cheek sites were covered, one fewer than
   required.
3. Extending the repair window by 40 steps left coverage unchanged at 13/16,
   rejecting insufficient maturation time as the cause.
4. Raising only within-region deficit contrast from 2.2 to 2.8 covered 14/16
   sites and passed. This value was frozen before seeds 19 and 31.

One batch sweep process exited after completing five of six validation runs;
the missing seed-31 learned run completed normally in isolation with finite
state and passed. This was an orchestration interruption, not a numerical
failure.

### Interpretation

H3 supports three claims. First, the particle state and renderer generalize
from five to 15 bones. Second, flesh volume can be specified independently of
nearest-bone distance: 45% of the target is meaningfully extra-skeletal, and
critical cheeks/buttocks retain high coverage during motion. Third, a tiny
shared MLP can replace the hand-written fate score without losing assembly or
remote repair.

The limitation is equally important. The MLP is supervised oracle distillation
and receives a global vector of oracle-defined regional shortage and
availability. It learns *which coarse fate to choose*, not how to discover
anatomy from local messages. Continuous transport, pressure, attachment,
maturation tests, and acceleration are still mechanical; the neural
acceleration residual is exactly zero. The procedural humanoid also lacks a
production joint hierarchy, skin surface chart, hands/face rig, and collision
model. H4 should import a real human rig and learn local flesh dynamics while
retaining H3 as its upper-bound teacher.
