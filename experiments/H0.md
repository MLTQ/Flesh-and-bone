# H0 — five-bone mechanical control

## Question

Can a fixed-capacity population of persistent Gaussian cells be fed from one
injection region, assemble a checkerboard body around the five-bone H scaffold,
differentiate permanently, retain two central negative spaces, and follow
bounded articulated motion?

## Method

- Five capsule bones: four vertical arms and one bridge.
- 299 regularly sampled developmental niches in the capsule union.
- 347 reservoir slots; eight cells activated per step until all niches reserve
  one cell.
- Cells enter below the H as orange, uncommitted splats.
- Damped niche attraction, Gaussian excess-density pressure, and short-range
  repulsion move the cells.
- Cells commit to a persistent navy/cream checker identity only after entering
  their reserved niche.
- Skeleton motion begins at step 230; the run ends at step 420.
- The shared particle-NCA residual exists but its final layer is exactly zero.

This is an explicit upper-bound control. Niche reservation supplies information
that the intended learned developmental system will not receive.

## Visual verdict

The animation clearly exposes the intended phases: orange cells enter below the
scaffold, migrate upward, switch to persistent checker identities, settle into a
volumetric H, and follow bending/spinning bones. Both negative spaces remain
open. Checker material moves with the body rather than being repainted in world
coordinates.

## What H0 proves

- The double-Y scaffold and soft bone-local embedding are internally consistent.
- Persistent splat identity survives articulated deformation.
- The fixed-capacity reservoir, feeding, commitment, renderer, metrics, and
  reproducible artifact pipeline function end to end.
- A sparse splat body makes surrounded empty space directly measurable.

## What H0 does not prove

- Density pressure is not yet causally necessary. Unique, regularly spaced
  niche targets already prevent final crowding.
- Cells do not choose a tissue from continuous morphogens; their destination is
  reserved at birth.
- The zero-output neural residual learns nothing.
- Birth/death, surplus rejection, damage recovery, and new-cell recruitment are
  not tested.

## Decision

Proceed to H1, but remove unique site assignments before investing in a larger
neural rule. H1 should rasterize current particle density onto a coarse field,
compare it with bone-conditioned tissue demand, and let uncommitted particles
follow the deficit gradient. The pressure-off and nearest-bone-only ablations
are required to determine whether density homeostasis—not hidden target
positions—preserves the H gaps.
