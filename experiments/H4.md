# H4 — production rig import and variable-thickness humanoid target

## Question

Can the Flesh and Bone substrate ingest a real skinned humanoid FBX, reproduce
its bind pose and 30-frame walking loop to numerical tolerance, preserve enough
skin influences for fine splats, and derive a watertight variable-thickness
developmental volume without reducing the body to bone capsules?

H4 is an asset/representation and transport experiment. The supplied walk has
ordinary linear skinning and no flesh mechanics, so it is not used as evidence
that secondary flesh motion has been learned.

## Source asset

`model/Meshy_AI_Blonde_female_mechani_biped.zip` contains:

- `Character_output.fbx`: canonical one-frame character, bind skeleton, mesh,
  UVs, weights, and material.
- `Animation_Walking_Woman_withSkin.fbx`: the same skinned character with an
  in-place walking take.
- Base-color, normal, metallic, and roughness textures.

The original archive is immutable input. Derived arrays and evidence must
record the source archive SHA-256 and extraction configuration.

## Stages and arms

### A — canonical extraction and skinning round-trip

Blender imports both FBXs and exports one compressed, renderer-independent
asset containing:

- canonical meter-space vertices and triangle topology;
- per-corner UVs and material texture references;
- ordered bone names, parents, bind matrices, and dense normalized weights;
- per-frame pose/skin matrices and Blender-evaluated mesh vertices;
- frame numbers, rate, axis transform, and provenance.

Arms compare full weights with normalized top-3, top-4, and top-6 weights. The
full-weight arm is the import correctness test; truncation arms determine the
smallest safe deployed state.

### B — fine animated surface splats

The rest mesh vertices become persistent surface cells carrying imported skin
weights and material samples. They are transported through the walk using the
selected influence representation and compared against Blender's evaluated
surface. The animation is an integration target, not a flesh-dynamics teacher.

### C — variable-thickness developmental target

Because the supplied mesh has small boundary/non-manifold defects, ordinary
signed inside/outside queries are not trusted. A pitch-controlled surface
voxelization is flood-filled to form a watertight occupancy field. Occupied
voxel centers inherit skin/material state from nearby source surface vertices.
This preserves head, cheek, chest, pelvis, and gluteal mass that lies far from
bone axes.

## Predeclared acceptance criteria

### Provenance and topology

- Source hash and both member names are recorded.
- Static and animated meshes have identical vertex/triangle counts and triangle
  indices.
- At least 20 weighted bones, no unweighted source vertices, normalized weight
  sum error at most `2e-5`, and at least one UV layer.
- Canonical height lies between 1.5 and 2.0 meters and feet are within 0.02 m of
  canonical ground.

### Full-weight skinning

- Rest-pose vertex RMS error at most `1e-6 m`.
- Across all animation frames, global RMS error at most `1e-5 m`, 99th
  percentile error at most `5e-5 m`, and maximum error at most `2e-4 m`
  relative to Blender-evaluated vertices.
- Frame-1/frame-30 skin-matrix RMS seam at most `1e-4`.

### Influence selection

- Report retained weight and animation error for top 3, 4, and 6.
- Select the smallest influence count with global RMS at most `5e-4 m`, 99th
  percentile at most `2e-3 m`, and maximum at most `1e-2 m`.
- If no truncated arm passes, retain all influences rather than weakening the
  gate.

### Surface and volume

- Animated surface arm uses every source vertex, has no non-finite state, and
  passes the selected-influence transport gate.
- Filled volume has at least 5,000 and at most 50,000 cells at pitch no greater
  than 0.025 m, one connected occupied component, and no enclosed empty pocket
  larger than 0.5% of occupied mass.
- At least 10% of volume lies more than 0.08 m from the nearest bone segment;
  the 95th-percentile bone distance is at least 0.12 m.
- The volume's largest rendered splat radius is no greater than H3's 0.0195 m.
- Imported/derived skin weights remain normalized and every volume cell has a
  valid dominant anatomical bone.

## Interpretation discipline

- Passing A proves FBX/axis/bind/animation correctness, not NCA behavior.
- Passing B proves fine persistent splats can follow a production rig, not that
  they self-assembled or exhibit secondary motion.
- Passing C establishes a usable variable-thickness target. Nearest-surface
  transfer is initialization privilege and must not be described as learned
  differentiation.
- Learned flesh residuals begin only after these controls pass and will require
  a separate teacher or physically motivated objective; the supplied linear
  skinned animation is insufficient for that claim.

## Results

### Source and canonical extraction

The source archive SHA-256 is
`1fd02f1e7ab1f6fb5771492145054020ca00cd84729be744f4767894dc59f188`.
Blender 5.1.2 imported the two FBXs and produced the versioned
`flesh-and-bone-h4-rig-v1` bundle. The static and animated files have identical
base vertices and topology:

| Measurement | Result |
|---|---:|
| Canonical vertices | 4,759 |
| Triangles | 9,553 |
| Bones / weighted bones | 24 / 22 |
| Unweighted vertices | 0 |
| Maximum source weight-sum error | `1.78e-7` |
| UV layers | 1 |
| Canonical height / ground | 1.700 m / `-5.6e-9` m |
| Static/animated bind vertex RMS | 0.0 m |
| Walk | frames 1–30 at 30 FPS |

The canonical transform maps Blender world `(x, y, z)` to experiment
`(x, z, -y)`, preserving a right-handed meter-space coordinate system with `y`
vertical.

### Skinning round-trip and influence arms

The full 24-slot weight state reproduces Blender's evaluated animation with
sub-micrometer error, establishing matrix order, inverse bind, axis, units, and
bone order:

| Influences | Mean retained weight | Minimum retained | RMS error | p99 error | Maximum error | Deployment gate |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|:---:|
| Full 24 slots | 1.000000 | 1.000000 | `1.10e-7` m | `3.51e-7` m | `6.26e-7` m | oracle pass |
| Top 3 | 0.979376 | 0.782474 | 0.002668 m | 0.010134 m | 0.035163 m | fail |
| Top 4 | 0.994556 | 0.897948 | 0.001062 m | 0.004766 m | 0.010675 m | fail |
| Top 6 | 0.999734 | 0.968780 | 0.000106 m | 0.000498 m | 0.002757 m | pass |

Top 6 is therefore frozen as the smallest deployed representation. Errors are
concentrated near multi-bone shoulder/torso seams rather than spread across the
body. The source's frame-1/frame-30 skin-matrix seam is `1.17e-5` RMS; resulting
top-6 surface points close at `4.61e-6` m RMS.

### Fine surface and variable-thickness volume

The surface arm carries all 4,759 source vertices as persistent textured
splats. The derived volume uses 0.025 m voxels:

| Measurement | Result | Gate |
|---|---:|---:|
| Filled cells | 13,273 | 5,000–50,000 |
| Grid | 33 × 69 × 21 | recorded |
| Occupied components | 1 | exactly 1 |
| Largest enclosed pocket | 0 cells | at most 0.5% mass |
| Extra-skeletal fraction (`>0.08 m`) | 0.620 | at least 0.10 |
| Bone-distance p95 / maximum | 0.190 / 0.290 m | p95 at least 0.12 m |
| Weight-sum maximum error | `2.22e-16` | at most `2e-5` |
| Material splat scale | 0.752–1.150 | variable |
| Maximum world splat radius | 0.008625 m | at most 0.0195 m |
| Walk-loop position seam | `4.32e-6` m RMS | finite/closed |

The original triangle mesh remains non-watertight, as declared. Surface
voxelization plus flood fill yields a single connected occupied body with no
enclosed empty component. Nearest-surface transfer supplies six-weight and UV
initialization to every volume cell; every dominant bone is valid.

### Iteration record

1. Binary FBX inspection established a standard 24-bone chain, 22 weighted
   groups, and a true 30-frame in-place loop.
2. The first canonical extractor passed full-weight round-trip immediately.
   Top-3 and top-4 truncation were rejected by the predeclared transport gates;
   top-6 passed.
3. Direct 0.025 m voxelization/fill produced 13,273 connected cells and passed
   every thickness/topology gate without morphology tuning.
4. The first evidence render used Gaussian radius `0.65 × pitch`. Metrics
   passed, but overlapping cells visually obscured the fine representation.
   Radius was tightened to `0.30 × pitch`; geometry, skinning, and gates were
   unchanged, while individual cells became visible. The stricter 0.008625 m
   maximum is frozen.

### Verdict

H4 passes every predeclared gate. We now have a production-rig-compatible,
Blender-independent 24-bone runtime asset, a measured six-influence skin state,
an exact animated surface oracle, and a connected variable-thickness 13k-cell
human target. Cheek, chest, pelvis, head, and gluteal thickness come from the
mesh/filled volume rather than nearest-bone radii.

This is not a flesh-mechanics result. The walking mesh is conventional linear
skinning, volume weights/UVs are transferred by a privileged nearest-surface
operation, and no NCA learned pressure, elasticity, incompressibility,
collision, or secondary motion. The appropriate next experiment is to drive a
soft-tissue teacher from the imported bones, then distill its local forces into
the particle-message rule while retaining H4 LBS as the kinematic baseline.
