# H5 — separately taught local flesh mechanics

## Question

Can a shared local particle-message rule learn stable secondary flesh motion
from a physically explicit teacher, rather than falsely treating H4's linear
skinned walk as flesh ground truth?

## Scope

H5 begins with one intentionally interpretable behavior class: small-strain,
under-damped elastic/inertial motion around the imported H4 LBS trajectory. It
does not yet claim collision, contact, plasticity, muscle activation, or large
deformation.

## Teacher

The H4 13,273-cell volume is connected by its six-neighbor voxel graph. Let
`q(t)` be the six-weight LBS position, `r = p - q` the flesh residual, and `u`
its velocity relative to LBS. The teacher integrates:

```text
u' = -k(d) r - c(k) u + g mean_neighbors(r_neighbor - r) - q''(t)
r' = u
```

- `d` is rest distance to the nearest bone.
- `k(d)` decreases with bone distance, so deep/bony material is firm and remote
  cheek, chest, pelvic, and gluteal material is soft.
- `c(k)` is under-critical damping.
- The graph term enforces coherent local residuals without a dense all-pairs
  interaction.
- `-q''` is inertial forcing from the animated skeleton.

The 30-frame FBX contains duplicate loop endpoints, so dynamics use the 29
unique phases at 30 Hz. Four substeps per phase and repeated warm-up cycles
produce a periodic teacher state.

This teacher is a controlled synthetic physics curriculum, not empirical human
soft-tissue ground truth.

## Learned rule and controls

One shared MLP predicts residual acceleration from per-cell local features:

- residual position and velocity;
- LBS equilibrium acceleration;
- mean neighbor residual and velocity differences;
- normalized bone distance and local stiffness.

The model is trained one-step on the converged teacher cycle, then evaluated
only by free autoregressive rollout for three cycles.

Arms:

1. **Teacher:** explicit graph-elastic dynamics.
2. **Learned local rule:** full features, autoregressive integration.
3. **LBS-only:** residual fixed to zero.
4. **Neighbor-blind:** identical learned weights with neighbor-difference
   features zeroed during rollout.

Only training seed 7 may be used for architecture/optimizer tuning. Seeds 19
and 31 validate frozen hyperparameters and thresholds.

## Predeclared acceptance criteria

### Teacher validity

- Six-neighbor graph has one component; every cell has at least one neighbor.
- All teacher states are finite over at least 30 warm-up cycles.
- Final cycle start/end residual-position RMS seam at most `5e-4 m`.
- Teacher residual RMS lies between `0.002 m` and `0.030 m`; LBS-only is
  therefore a nontrivial but bounded control.
- Mean residual amplitude of cells farther than 0.12 m from bone is at least
  1.35 times that of cells nearer than 0.05 m.
- Neighbor-edge residual difference RMS remains below `0.020 m`.

### Learned rule

- Held-out one-step acceleration normalized RMSE at most 0.15.
- Three-cycle autoregressive position RMS at most `0.004 m`, p99 at most
  `0.012 m`, and maximum at most `0.040 m` relative to the teacher.
- Learned residual RMS amplitude is between 0.75 and 1.25 of the teacher.
- Learned far/near amplitude ratio is at least 1.25.
- Per-cycle phase-zero drift is at most `0.003 m` RMS and all state is finite.
- The neighbor-blind rollout has at least 20% larger RMS error or at least 25%
  larger edge-strain error than the full learned rollout.
- Learned rollout beats LBS-only RMS error by at least 35%.

### Reproducibility

- Frozen seeds 7, 19, and 31 all pass.
- Every run records teacher parameters, graph statistics, optimizer settings,
  checkpoint, metrics, and visual artifacts.

## Interpretation discipline

Passing H5 demonstrates that a shared local rule can distill this particular
graph-elastic teacher and remain stable under rollout. It does not establish
that the teacher is biologically accurate, nor that the rule generalizes to
unseen bodies, contacts, garments, or motions. Those require separate controls
and data.

## Results

### Frozen teacher

The 13,273 cells form one connected graph with 72,252 directed edges and no
isolates. The teacher reaches a `1.73e-9 m` cycle seam after warm-up. Its
residual motion is nontrivial but bounded: `22.813 mm` RMS with a `2.169`
far/near amplitude ratio and `4.710 mm` neighbor-edge difference RMS. It passes
every teacher gate.

### Trial history

The first frozen 1,200-step run was retained at
`experiments/runs/h5_failed_1200`. Seeds 7 and 19 passed, but seed 31 reached a
`44.71 mm` maximum position error against the predeclared `40 mm` gate despite
only `0.978 mm` RMS and `3.862 mm` p99 error. Diagnosis localized the maximum
to one rare degree-2 soft-surface cell, 0.182 m from bone; the degree-6 interior
remained below `20.41 mm` maximum error. The failure was therefore treated as
insufficient exposure of the sparse boundary tail, not hidden by relaxing the
gate.

The only recovery change was doubling optimizer steps from 1,200 to 2,400.
On the permitted seed-7 tuning arm this reduced RMS from `0.906 mm` to
`0.511 mm` and maximum error from `30.32 mm` to `16.81 mm`, without changing
teacher physics, graph, features, model width, controls, or thresholds. That
budget was then frozen and all three declared seeds were rerun from scratch.

### Final three-seed result

| Seed | Holdout accel NRMSE | Rollout RMS | p99 | max | cycle drift | LBS error removed |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 7 | 0.0253 | 0.513 mm | 2.062 mm | 16.843 mm | 0.500 mm | 97.75% |
| 19 | 0.0248 | 0.528 mm | 2.178 mm | 19.619 mm | 0.470 mm | 97.69% |
| 31 | 0.0222 | 0.478 mm | 1.879 mm | 16.051 mm | 0.456 mm | 97.90% |

All seeds pass every predeclared gate. Learned motion retains `0.998-0.999`
of teacher RMS amplitude and a `2.163-2.167` far/near ratio. With neighbor
features removed from the identical checkpoints, position error becomes
`3.88-4.19x` worse and edge-strain error becomes `5.31-5.89x` worse. The rule
is therefore using local cell messages causally; good performance is not just a
per-cell response to bone acceleration.

Actual-scale and explicitly labeled `4x` residual renders were visually
inspected. The learned rollout stays coherent with the teacher through the walk
cycle; the exaggerated view makes the soft-tissue lag legible without being
misrepresented as physical scale. Canonical evidence is in
`experiments/runs/h5_final`.

## Decision

Keep the local message-rule path. H5 establishes stable distillation of one
interpretable graph-elastic behavior over a production rig and supplies a
meaningful LBS and neighbor-blind causal comparison. It does **not** establish
biological realism or motion/body generalization: training and evaluation use
the same walk and the same topology. The next mechanics experiment should
freeze this rule and test novel skeletal accelerations, then add density,
contact, and large-deformation curricula one mechanism at a time.
