# H1 — continuous tissue-deficit recruitment

## Question

Can freely fed mobile cells assemble around the five-bone H, preserve its two
surrounded negative spaces, differentiate, and follow articulation without any
cell receiving or retaining a target-site identity?

## Method

- The body plan is the same 299-sample capsule-union H used in H0.
- Four unassigned cells enter per step from below until 299 cells are active.
- Target samples rasterize continuous tissue demand and measurement only. No
  sample is reserved and every particle's `assigned_site` remains `-1`.
- Current particle occupancy produces a live deficit at each field sample.
- Cells attend only within a narrow local field (`1.3 × spacing`) so symmetric
  H branches cannot average into an invalid central destination.
- Excess local density produces pressure; short-range overlap produces stronger
  repulsion.
- Contact begins an attached but materially plastic state. During stationary
  assembly the cell reads checker phenotype from its nearest local sample of the
  material field; the sample index is not stored or reserved.
- At motion onset all contacted material locks. Checker phenotype and the fresh
  three-bone soft embedding then remain persistent throughout articulation.
- During articulation, committed cells follow that embedding with only a 5%
  live-deficit correction.
- H1 splats use a world-space Gaussian radius of `0.20 × spacing` versus H0's
  `0.31 × spacing`, a 35% reduction in linear cell size.
- The learned NCA residual remains exactly zero. H1 tests the developmental
  substrate before learning is added.

## Iteration record

The first normalized field used a `4.2 × spacing` recruitment radius. It failed:
wide attention averaged the left and right under-filled branches into a central
blob (seed 7 moving coverage 0.562; 244/299 committed). This was not a capacity
problem. It was a vector-field topology error.

Narrowing the attention below branch separation produced the H. A `1.5 ×`
trial passed seed 7 but missed the moving-coverage gate on seeds 19 and 31.
The final `1.3 ×` field and reduced moving correction improved motion retention;
no threshold was changed during tuning.

### Checker-collapse investigation

The original H1 result passed `checker_balance_error` because it contained
roughly the right total number of navy and cream cells, yet its spatial pattern
had visibly collapsed. That metric was only a histogram and could not distinguish
a checker from a scrambled or depth-segregated arrangement.

A new predeclared gate measures checker agreement at covered body-field samples
and requires at least 0.80 accuracy. The old first-contact phenotype scores only
0.561 on seed 7. Three mechanisms were separated:

1. Re-evaluating a hard analytic XYZ checker at each cell's continuous position
   reaches only 0.557 after motion. Free particles often cross discontinuous
   parity boundaries before lock.
2. Reading the nearest local XYZ material sample reaches 0.886, but the render
   remains speckled because front/back parity layers occlude one another.
3. Defining the skin on a canonical X/Y chart, extruded through thickness, and
   reading that local field before lock reaches 0.923 and is visibly coherent.

The third rule is the final H1 material contract. It is not world-space
repainting: phenotype is plastic only during stationary development and is
frozen before the skeleton moves. A general 3D creature will need persistent
bone-surface UV coordinates; an XYZ voxel checker is not a surface texture.

## Causal controls, seed 7

| Arm | Moving coverage | Density error | Crowding | Part balance error | Pass |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|:---:|
| Deficit + pressure | 0.906 | 0.104 | 0.00018 | 0.0435 | yes |
| Deficit, pressure off | 0.110 | 1.719 | 0.11490 | 0.2809 | no |
| Nearest bone + pressure | 0.957 | 0.048 | 0.00009 | 0.0268 | yes |

Pressure is necessary: without it, cells attach near the first reachable lower
bone regions, pile into dense clumps, and leave most of the H empty.

Nearest-bone attraction is unexpectedly sufficient here. That is a useful
negative result. The target is a union of nearly uniform-radius capsules, so
nearest-bone attraction plus pressure already describes its equilibrium well.
H1 does **not** yet prove that the richer deficit field is necessary. A later
test needs spatially varying tissue thickness, nonuniform target density, damage
and re-feeding, or a shape whose desired surface is not a uniform bone offset.

## Decision

Keep the mobile splat substrate and density-pressure prior. H1 meaningfully
removes H0's one-cell/one-niche privilege and produces coherent moving tissue
with smaller visible cells. Two-stage plastic-to-locked phenotype is required;
first-contact identity is not stable enough during self-assembly. Do not yet
claim learned NCA behavior or general deficit-driven morphogenesis. The next
discriminating experiment should add a nonuniform body envelope, true
bone-surface coordinates, and patch deletion/re-feeding before training the
neural residual.
